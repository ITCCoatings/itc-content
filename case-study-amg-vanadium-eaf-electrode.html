Facility & Challenge

AMG Vanadium operates a high-intensity electric arc furnace in Cambridge, Ohio producing vanadium-bearing alloys. The EAF process subjects graphite electrodes to extreme oxidation, thermal cycling, and chemical attack, resulting in rapid electrode degradation and side-wall burning. This accelerated consumption was driving up raw material costs and causing unplanned production interruptions.

The facility was experiencing above-average electrode consumption rates compared to industry benchmarks for similar heat profiles. Electrode costs represent one of the largest variable operating expenses in EAF steelmaking, and any reduction yields compounding savings across high production volumes.

ITC Solution

ITC Coatings recommended application of ITC 213 ceramic coating to the electrode side walls above the slag line. ITC 213 is a high-emissivity, oxidation-resistant ceramic coating formulated specifically for graphite and carbon-based substrates in extreme-temperature oxidizing environments.

The coating was applied by brush to clean electrode surfaces prior to furnace charging. ITC 213 forms a protective ceramic barrier that:

  • Reduces oxidation of the graphite electrode at high temperatures
  • Reflects radiant heat back into the melt, improving energy efficiency
  • Protects against chemical attack from slag and off-gases
  • Reduces side-wall burning above the slag line
