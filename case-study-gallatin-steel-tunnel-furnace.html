Facility & Challenge

Gallatin Steel is a flat-rolled steel producer operating one of the most modern thin-slab casting and rolling facilities in North America. Their operation includes a tunnel furnace (the continuous furnace connecting the caster to the rolling mill) that maintains the cast slab at rolling temperature throughout the tunnel before entering the hot strip mill.

Key operational challenges for the tunnel furnace included:

  • High continuous fuel consumption — the tunnel furnace operates 24/7 to maintain rolling temperature
  • Scale formation on slab surfaces during transit through the tunnel furnace
  • Refractory lining surfaces with degraded emissivity, reducing efficiency of radiant heat transfer
  • Non-uniform temperature distribution along the tunnel length

ITC Solution

During a scheduled maintenance outage, ITC Coatings applied ITC 100HT to the entire interior surface of the tunnel furnace — roof, sidewalls, and available floor areas — covering several thousand square feet of refractory and ceramic fiber surfaces.

The high-emissivity coating (0.92+) provides:

  • More efficient radiant heat transfer from the furnace lining to the steel slab
  • Reduced fuel input required to maintain the same slab temperature setpoints
  • More uniform temperature distribution along the tunnel length
  • Sealed refractory surface resisting scale accumulation

  • ITC 100HT delivered 8–12% natural gas savings at Gallatin Steel's tunnel furnace
