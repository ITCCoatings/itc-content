Facility & Challenge

North American Forgemasters operates a large forging furnace approximately 32' L × 32' W × 13' H, with a ceramic fiber roof, upper sidewalls, and door — with hard cast lower sidewall lining. The furnace runs at 2,200°F – 2,300°F and uses 12" pyroblock ceramic fiber module and hard cast construction.

At these extreme temperatures, ceramic fiber linings are subject to:

  • Shrinkage and devitrification — fibers crystallize and lose insulating properties over time
  • Elevated shell temperatures and hot spots — indicating heat loss and lining degradation
  • High refractory maintenance frequency — material and labor costs for continual repair
  • Extended turn-around times after maintenance, reducing productive tonnage per week

ITC Solution

ITC 100HT

ITC 100HT was applied to the hot face of the ceramic fiber roof, upper sidewalls, and door lining. The coating performs two critical functions:

  • Protection: Creates a hard ceramic barrier that prevents fiber shrinkage and devitrification, dramatically extending lining life
  • Reflection: Reflects radiant heat back into the working zone, improving BTU efficiency and lowering shell temperatures

  • 3–5x refractory life — the single biggest cost driver in a high-temp forge furnace. Extending life 3–5x transforms the economics of the operation.
  • 20–30% faster turn-around — translates directly to additional forging tonnage produced per week with the same equipment
  • Shell temperature reduction — confirms heat is staying in the working zone instead of escaping through the lining
  • Applicable to all ceramic fiber lined forging furnaces — car bottom furnaces, box furnaces, rotary hearth — any furnace with ceramic fiber at 1,800°F+
