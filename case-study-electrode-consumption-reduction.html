Facility & Challenge

The Defense Logistics Agency's ManTech Forging Program evaluated ceramic coatings on graphite electrodes in Electric Arc Furnaces (EAFs) used in defense-critical steel production. The challenge: electrode consumption rates were running above industry standard on 24" diameter electrodes.

Operating parameters:

  • Electrode diameter: 24 inches
  • Average heat times: 2.5 – 3 hours
  • Tap temperatures: 2,990°F – 3,000°F

High electrode consumption rates created cascading problems: frequent electrode additions requiring crane time, maintenance downtime, and stray electric arcs from conductivity loss — all impacting throughput and operating margins.

ITC Solution

ITC 303EC

The program designed and implemented a fully automated spraying system to apply ITC ceramic coating to EAF graphite electrodes. The coating was applied via automated spray before the electrodes entered service.

The ceramic coating works by:

  • Protecting the graphite electrode surface from oxidation at extreme arc temperatures
  • Reducing the oxidation cone that forms at the electrode tip — the primary source of consumption
  • Channeling electrical current through the electrode tip — improving conductivity and eliminating stray arcs
  • Extending the effective tip diameter longer, reducing tip loss rate

Key Takeaways

  • 25–30% electrode consumption reduction — graphite electrodes are among the most expensive consumables in EAF steelmaking. A 25–30% reduction is significant operating cost savings.
  • More heats per month — less time waiting for electrode additions = more productive arc-on time
  • Validated by DLA ManTech program — government-funded, independently validated study using rigorous before/after metrics
  • Automated application system developed — scalable to any EAF operation
  • Directly applicable to: Charter Steel, Cleveland Cliffs, Gerdau, AMG Vanadium, and any other EAF operation (see companion electrode case studies)
  • ITC 303EC electrode coating is a compelling standalone offering for EAF operators — different product, same ITC quality
