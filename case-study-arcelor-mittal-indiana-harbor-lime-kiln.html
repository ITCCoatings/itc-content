Facility & Challenge

ArcelorMittal's Indiana Harbor Works operates one of the largest integrated steel complexes in North America. The facility includes rotary lime kilns that calcine limestone to produce quicklime — an essential flux used throughout the steelmaking process.

The lime kilns operate continuously at temperatures exceeding 2,000°F and consume large volumes of natural gas or other fuel. Key challenges included:

  • High fuel consumption relative to lime production output (high specific energy)
  • Refractory brick lining absorbing and radiating heat outward rather than inward toward the product
  • Reduced throughput capacity due to temperature recovery time between cycles
  • Significant heat loss through kiln shell detected via thermal imaging

ITC Solution

ITC Coatings applied ITC 100HT high-emissivity ceramic coating to the interior refractory lining of the rotary lime kiln. The coating transforms the low-emissivity refractory brick surface into a high-emissivity radiator (0.92+ emissivity vs ~0.3 for bare refractory).

Application was performed during a scheduled maintenance turnaround. The coating was sprayed uniformly across the refractory brick interior, covering approximately 8,000–12,000 square feet of kiln interior surface area.

The high-emissivity surface radiates absorbed energy back toward the limestone charge rather than allowing it to conduct through the refractory and escape as shell heat loss. This dramatically improves the thermal efficiency of the calcination process.
