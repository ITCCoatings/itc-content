Facility & Challenge

Charter Steel operates electric arc furnaces producing specialty bar quality steel for demanding applications. The EAF process presents two critical wear challenges that ITC was asked to address in a controlled coating trial:

1. Electrode Oxidation: Graphite electrodes oxidize at high rates during EAF operation, particularly above the slag line where oxygen is present. Side-wall oxidation removes electrode material without contributing energy to the melt.

2. Delta Section Wear: The delta section (the triangular structural component supporting electrode clamps and arms) is exposed to the harsh thermal and chemical environment inside the furnace shell. Premature wear of this component necessitates costly and time-consuming repairs.

ITC Solution

A structured trial was designed to apply ITC 213 to:

  • Electrode side walls above the slag line — to reduce oxidation-driven consumption
  • Delta section surfaces — to protect against thermal and chemical attack

ITC 213 is an alumina-silica ceramic coating that bonds to both graphite and metal substrates, creating an oxygen-impermeable barrier at high temperatures. The coating was applied by brush prior to each heat cycle during the trial period.

Data was collected over multiple heats comparing coated vs. uncoated electrode consumption rates and delta section wear observations.
