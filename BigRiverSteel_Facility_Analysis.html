Facility Overview

Big River Steel Works (a U.S. Steel company) operates one of the most advanced EAF flat-rolled steel facilities in North America, located in Osceola, Arkansas. With the completion of the Big River 2 (BR2) expansion in late 2025, the combined campus has an annual capacity of 6 million tons across four EAFs, producing flat-rolled products for automotive, energy, construction, electrical steel, and agriculture markets.

The facility includes EAFs, ladle metallurgy, continuous casting, hot strip mill, cold rolling, continuous galvanizing lines, batch annealing furnaces, and a new electrical steel line — presenting 13 distinct application areas for ITC coatings.

1. Electric Arc Furnaces (4 EAFs)

Big River operates four EAFs — two in the original plant (3M t/yr) and two in the BR2 expansion (3M t/yr). Combined capacity: 6 million tons/year of liquid steel.

1A. EAF Roof & Delta

ITC 100HT ITC 296A

The EAF delta is the refractory insert in the center of the furnace roof through which electrodes enter. It faces extreme arc radiation, thermal shock, and chemical attack from slag and off-gas. Delta life directly impacts furnace uptime.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on the refractory delta hot face protects against thermal shock and extends delta life. ITC 296A top coat resists slag deposits and buildup.

Scale at Big River: 4 EAFs × 1 delta each = 4 deltas

Reference: Charter Steel EAF Delta Coating Trial — proven delta life extension

1B. EAF Electrodes

ITC 303EC

Graphite electrodes are among the most expensive consumables in EAF steelmaking. Oxidation at the electrode surface creates a consumption cone that accelerates electrode loss. Big River operates 24" diameter electrodes across 4 furnaces with tap temperatures of 2,990–3,000°F.

ITC Solution: ITC 303EC ceramic electrode coating reduces the oxidation cone, extends tip diameter life, channels electrical current through the tip (improving conductivity and eliminating stray arcs), and reduces electrode additions — decreasing crane time and maintenance downtime.

Scale at Big River: 4 EAFs × continuous electrode consumption = massive annual spend on graphite electrodes

Reference: DLA ManTech Program — 25–30% electrode consumption reduction (government-validated). Also: AMG Vanadium, Cleveland-Cliffs Mansfield, Gerdau Monroe — all confirmed electrode savings.

1C. EAF Off-Gas Ductwork

ITC 213 ITC 100HT ITC 296A

EAF off-gas ducts carry extremely hot exhaust (1,200–1,500°C) containing metallic dust, acid compounds, and corrosive condensation at the acid dew point on cooler duct sections. Steel ductwork corrodes from the inside out.

ITC Solution: Three-coat system: ITC 213 on metal surfaces + ITC 100HT thermal barrier + ITC 296A top coat. Prevents acid dew point corrosion and extends ductwork life — identical to the proven TXI Cement bag house application where steel shell life was extended 4–5x.

Reference: TXI Cement bag house — 4–5x steel shell life extension

2. Ladle Metallurgy

Big River operates multiple ladle treatment stands for secondary metallurgy — alloying, desulfurization, temperature adjustment, and inclusion removal before continuous casting. With 6M tons/year throughput, ladle cycling is intense.

2A. Ladle Shell Protection (Breakout Prevention)

ITC 213

Carbon from high-carbon steel can diffuse into the low-carbon steel ladle shell, lowering the melting point to the eutectic and causing catastrophic breakouts — a serious safety hazard that can injure workers, destroy equipment, and halt production.

ITC Solution: ITC ceramic coating on the inside of the ladle steel shell before refractory installation prevents steel-shell contact, eliminates carbon diffusion, and stops breakouts.

Reference: ITC Molten Iron Breakout Prevention tech bulletin — SAFETY-CRITICAL application

2B. Ladle Covers & Preheaters

ITC 100HT

Ladle covers and preheater lids are typically lined with ceramic fiber that faces severe thermal cycling. Fiber shrinks and devitrifies over thousands of cycles, requiring frequent replacement.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on the ceramic fiber hot face prevents shrinkage and devitrification, extends cover life 3–5x, and improves heat retention during ladle transport — maintaining steel temperature for better casting quality.

Scale at Big River: Multiple ladle covers and preheaters across both plants

Reference: McConway & Torley — 3–5x refractory life, 46% improvement in temperature uniformity, $480K/yr additional revenue. EVRAZ Rocky Mountain ladle preheat lid — extended lid life.

2C. Ladle Porous Plug Extractors

ITC 213

Porous plug extractors are metal components used to remove and replace argon stirring plugs in ladle bottoms. They face extreme heat and oxidation during each plug change cycle.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 on porous plug extractors provides oxidation and thermal protection, extending component life and reducing replacement frequency.

Reference: EVRAZ Rocky Mountain Ladle Porous Plug Extractor case study

2D. RH Vacuum Degasser

ITC 100HT ITC 213

The RH (Ruhrstahl-Heraeus) vacuum degasser circulates molten steel through a vacuum chamber to remove dissolved gases (hydrogen, nitrogen, carbon) for ultra-low carbon grades, automotive AHSS, and electrical steel. The vessel refractory faces extreme conditions: molten steel contact, vacuum cycling, thermal shock, and chemical attack from decarburization reactions. The snorkels (immersion legs) and vessel roof are particularly high-wear areas.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on the vessel refractory and roof protects against thermal shock and extends campaign life. ITC 213 on metal components (vessel shell, snorkel cooling systems, off-gas ductwork) provides oxidation and corrosion protection.

Scale at Big River: RH degasser(s) processing specialty grades including automotive AHSS and electrical steel — high-value product lines where vessel uptime directly impacts revenue

Critical for Big River's premium product mix — electrical steel (InduX™) and automotive AHSS both require RH degassing

3. Hot Strip Mill — Tunnel Furnaces

Big River's hot strip mill processes slabs from the casters into hot-rolled coil. The BR2 expansion introduced an endless casting and rolling line — a first in the U.S. — integrating the caster directly with the hot mill via tunnel furnaces.

3A. Tunnel Furnaces

ITC 100HT ITC 296A

Tunnel furnaces maintain slab temperature between casting and rolling, operating continuously at high temperatures. The refractory linings — typically ceramic fiber or IFB — face sustained heat exposure, thermal cycling during grade changes, and scale buildup from the steel slabs passing through.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on the hot face of the tunnel furnace refractory reflects radiant heat back to the slab (improving temperature uniformity), prevents fiber shrinkage and devitrification, and extends refractory lining life. ITC 296A top coat resists scale and oxide deposit buildup on the lining surface.

Scale at Big River: Multiple tunnel furnaces across original plant and BR2 endless casting line

Reference: Gallatin Steel Tunnel Furnace Impact case study — proven tunnel furnace application with energy savings and refractory life extension

4. Continuous Casting

Big River operates multiple continuous casters producing slabs for the hot strip mill. The BR2 expansion introduced an endless casting and rolling line — a first in the U.S.

4A. Tundish Covers & Preheaters

ITC 100HT

Tundish covers are ceramic fiber lined and face thermal cycling with every heat. Fiber degradation requires frequent replacement — a significant maintenance cost in high-throughput casting operations.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on ceramic fiber extends tundish cover life 2–3x, reduces heat loss to the tundish, and maintains steel temperature for better casting quality and fewer nozzle clog events.

4B. Tundish Shell Protection

ITC 213

Tundish steel shells face the same carbon diffusion/breakout risk as ladles when refractory is penetrated by molten steel.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 on the tundish shell interior before refractory lining prevents breakouts.

Reference: ITC Molten Iron Breakout Prevention tech bulletin

5. Galvanizing Lines (4+ Lines)

Big River operates multiple continuous galvanizing lines (CGLs) — including a combined CAL/CGL in the original plant and three new CGLs in the BR2 expansion. These lines apply zinc coatings to flat-rolled steel for automotive and construction markets.

5A. Galvanizing Snouts

ITC 213 ITC 296A

The galvanizing snout is the submerged tube that guides the steel strip into the molten zinc bath. It faces continuous attack from zinc vapor, dross buildup, and extreme thermal gradients. Snout degradation causes zinc coating quality defects and requires costly shutdowns for replacement.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 on the snout metal surface protects against zinc attack and oxidation. ITC 296A top coat resists dross and zinc vapor deposit buildup, extending snout campaign life and reducing shutdown frequency.

Scale at Big River: 4+ galvanizing lines × 1 snout each = 4+ snouts

Reference: Big River Steel Galvanizing Snout Impact case study — ITC already installed and proven at this facility

6. Batch Annealing Furnaces

Big River operates batch annealing furnaces for both stainless and carbon steel grades. These furnaces use ceramic fiber linings and operate at temperatures that cause fiber degradation over time.

6A. Batch Anneal Furnace Linings

ITC 100HT

Batch annealing furnaces cycle between ambient and operating temperature with every batch. Ceramic fiber linings shrink and devitrify under repeated thermal cycling, increasing heat loss and requiring frequent maintenance.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on the hot face of ceramic fiber linings prevents shrinkage and devitrification, reflects radiant heat back into the working zone for better temperature uniformity, and extends lining life 3–5x.

Scale at Big River: Multiple batch annealing furnaces across stainless and carbon sides

Reference: McConway & Torley — 3–5x refractory life, 10% fuel reduction, 46% improvement in temperature uniformity. North American Forgemasters — 3–5x refractory life, 20–30% faster turn-around.

7. Electrical Steel Line (NGO — InduX™)

Big River's new $450M electrical steel line (operational Q3 2024) produces non-grain oriented (NGO) electrical steel for EV motors, generators, and transformers at 200,000 tons/year. A grain-oriented electrical steel line is also planned.

7A. Electrical Steel Annealing Furnaces

ITC 100HT

NGO electrical steel requires precise annealing at controlled temperatures to achieve the magnetic properties that define product quality. Furnace temperature uniformity is critical — any hot spots or cold zones create inconsistent grain structure and degrade magnetic performance.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on furnace linings improves temperature uniformity (proven 46% improvement at McConway & Torley) and reduces energy consumption — both directly impacting electrical steel product quality and manufacturing cost.

This is a premium product line — quality improvements have outsized revenue impact
