Facility Overview

HKM operates one of Europe's largest integrated steel plants in Duisburg-Huckingen, producing 5-6 million metric tons of steel annually across 2,000+ grades. The facility includes coking plants, sinter facilities, blast furnaces, BOF converters, ladle metallurgy, continuous casting, and hot rolling mills with reheat furnaces -- every stage of which presents ITC coating applications.

This document identifies 18 distinct application areas where ITC coatings can reduce energy consumption, extend equipment life, prevent breakouts, and lower maintenance costs.

1. Coking Plant

HKM operates two coke oven batteries (140 ovens total, each 70 m3 effective volume) producing 2.32 million tonnes of coke per year. The coking plant is the application Christian from HKM knows best.

1A. Coke Oven Doors

ITC 100HT ITC 296A

Coke oven doors face extreme thermal cycling (ambient to 1,100 deg.C+) every coking cycle. Door refractory and sealing surfaces degrade from thermal shock, causing emissions and requiring frequent maintenance.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on door refractory protects against thermal shock and spalling, extending door refractory life 2-3x. ITC 296A top coat resists carbon and tar deposits.

Scale at HKM: 140 oven doors x 2 sides = 280 door faces

Proven: Greg has coking oven door photos and case data to share

1B. Oven Chamber Walls

ITC 100HT

Silica brick oven walls operate at 1,000-1,100 deg.C and degrade over decades of thermal cycling. Wall thinning reduces oven life and eventually requires battery rebuild (hundreds of millions in capital).

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on hot face of silica brick walls protects against thermal shock, reduces heat loss through walls, and extends battery campaign life.

Scale at HKM: 140 ovens, each 18m long x 7.85m high

1C. Ascension Pipes and Collecting Mains

ITC 213 ITC 296A

Ascension pipes and collecting mains carry hot coke oven gas (600-800 deg.C) and are subject to tar/carbon buildup, corrosion, and thermal fatigue.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 on metal surfaces protects against corrosion and oxidation. ITC 296A top coat resists deposit buildup from coke oven gas.

2. Sinter Plant

2A. Sinter Strand Wind Boxes and Ductwork

ITC 213 ITC 100HT ITC 296A

Sinter plant ductwork and wind boxes face high-temperature corrosive exhaust gases containing sulfur compounds and alkali chlorides. Acid dew point corrosion attacks steel components.

ITC Solution: Three-coat system (213 + 100HT + 296A) prevents acid dew point corrosion on steel ductwork -- identical to the proven TXI Cement bag house application where steel shell life was extended 4-5x.

Reference: TXI Cement bag house case study -- 4-5x steel shell life extension

2B. Sinter Plant Refractory Anchors

ITC 213

Stainless steel refractory anchors in the sinter plant corrode from alkali chlorides and sulphates in the exhaust gas.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 coating on anchors before installation. Proven in testing: every steel grade improved at least one quality level. Uncoated Inconel 601 destroyed; ITC-coated retained structure.

Reference: ITC Anchor Corrosion Testing -- 100% improvement rate on all 7 steel grades tested

3. Blast Furnaces (A and B)

Two blast furnaces with hearth diameters up to 11m, combined capacity of 4-5 million tonnes of hot metal per year.

3A. Hot Blast Stoves

ITC 100HT ITC 296A

Hot blast stoves preheat combustion air to 1,100-1,300 deg.C. The refractory dome and checker bricks face extreme thermal cycling every blast/gas cycle.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on hot face of stove dome and upper checker courses protects refractory from thermal shock and reduces heat loss, improving blast temperature and fuel efficiency.

3B. Blast Furnace Tuyeres and Tuyere Area

ITC 213

The tuyere area operates at extreme temperatures with direct exposure to molten iron and slag. Metal components face oxidation and erosion.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 on tuyere coolers and surrounding metal components provides oxidation and erosion protection, extending component life.

3C. Cast House Runners and Troughs

ITC 100HT ITC 296A

Iron and slag runners carry molten iron at 1,400-1,500 deg.C. Refractory linings erode rapidly from molten metal contact and thermal shock.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT + 296A on hot face of runner refractory reduces erosion, extends campaign life, and improves iron flow characteristics.

4. Torpedo Cars

4A. Torpedo Car Steel Shell Protection

ITC 213

HKM uses 260-tonne torpedo cars to transport molten iron from blast furnaces to BOF. Carbon from high-carbon iron diffuses into the low-carbon steel shell, lowering the melting point to the eutectic and causing breakouts -- a serious safety hazard.

ITC Solution: ITC ceramic coating on inside of steel shell before refractory installation prevents iron-steel contact, eliminates carbon diffusion, and stops breakouts.

Reference: ITC Molten Iron Breakout Prevention tech bulletin -- this is a SAFETY-CRITICAL application

Scale at HKM: Multiple torpedo cars in rotation, each 260 tonnes capacity

5. BOF Steelmaking

Two 300-tonne LD converters with change vessel system.

5A. BOF Off-Gas Ductwork

ITC 213 ITC 100HT

BOF off-gas ducts carry extremely hot gases (1,500 deg.C+) containing metallic dust and corrosive compounds. Water-cooled ductwork sections face oxidation, thermal fatigue, and deposit buildup.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 on metal surfaces plus ITC 100HT thermal barrier protects ductwork, reduces deposit adhesion, and extends component life.

6. Ladle Metallurgy

HKM has 285-tonne ladle treatment stands, twin-tank vacuum degassers, and argon bubbling stands processing 5.2 million tonnes of liquid steel per year.

6A. Steel Ladle Shell Protection

ITC 213

Same carbon diffusion/breakout prevention as torpedo cars. Molten steel can penetrate refractory and contact the ladle shell, with catastrophic consequences.

ITC Solution: ITC coating on inside of ladle shell before refractory installation prevents breakouts.

Reference: ITC Molten Iron Breakout Prevention tech bulletin

6B. Ladle Covers and Preheaters

ITC 100HT

Ladle covers and preheating stations face severe thermal cycling. Ceramic fiber linings in covers shrink and degrade.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on ceramic fiber prevents shrinkage and devitrification, extends cover life, and reduces heat loss during transport.

Reference: McConway and Torley -- 3-5x refractory life, 46% improvement in temperature uniformity

7. Continuous Casting

Five continuous casters (11 round bar strands + 8 slab strands), producing slabs and round blooms.

7A. Tundish Covers and Preheaters

ITC 100HT

Tundish covers are typically ceramic fiber lined and face thermal cycling with every heat. Fiber degradation requires frequent replacement.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on ceramic fiber extends cover life 2-3x and reduces heat loss to the tundish, maintaining steel temperature for better casting quality.

7B. Tundish Shell Protection

ITC 213

Tundish steel shells face the same carbon diffusion risk as ladles when refractory is penetrated by molten steel.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 on tundish shell interior before refractory lining prevents breakouts.

8. Hot Rolling Mill — Reheat Furnaces

HKM produces slabs and round blooms via five continuous casters, which are then rolled in the hot mill. Slabs and blooms must be reheated to rolling temperature (1,200–1,300 deg.C) in reheat furnaces before entering the rolling stands. These furnaces are among the highest-consumption energy users in the facility.

8A. Reheat Furnace Refractory — Roof, Sidewalls, and Hearth

ITC 100HT ITC 296A

Reheat furnaces (walking beam or pusher type) operate continuously at 1,200–1,300 deg.C. The refractory linings — ceramic fiber roof and sidewalls, and hard-face hearth — face sustained high temperatures, thermal cycling during production changes, and scale buildup from the steel slabs.

ITC Solution: ITC 100HT on ceramic fiber roof and sidewalls prevents shrinkage and devitrification, reflects radiant heat back into the working zone, and extends refractory life 3–5x. ITC 296A top coat resists scale and oxide deposits on the hot face. On hard-face refractory, ITC 100HT seals and protects against thermal shock.

Reference: Nucor Steel Birmingham — $1,230,000/yr fuel savings, 54.7 MM BTU/hr. ArcelorMittal Harriman — $3,762,500/yr total impact. SDI Pittsboro — $33,857/yr per skid pipe.

8B. Reheat Furnace Skid Pipes

ITC 100HT

Walking beam and pusher reheat furnaces use water-cooled skid pipes to support and transport slabs through the furnace. The skid pipes create "skid marks" — cold spots on the slab underside — causing temperature non-uniformity that affects rolling quality. The skid pipe insulation (veneer system — ceramic fiber squares bonded to the pipe) degrades from thermal cycling and mechanical contact with slabs.

ITC Solution: ITC veneer system (ceramic fiber squares + ITC coating) on skid pipes reduces the temperature differential (Delta T) across the slab by 50–80%, dramatically improving slab temperature uniformity for rolling. This translates directly to fuel savings, reduced scale loss, and better surface quality.

Scale at HKM: Multiple reheat furnaces × multiple skid pipe rows per furnace

Reference: SDI Pittsboro — Delta T dropped from 26–38 deg.F to 7–13 deg.F (50–80% reduction). $33,857/year savings on ONE skid pipe. Nucor Birmingham — $1,230,000/yr fuel savings with ITC veneer system on roof, sidewalls, and skid pipes.

8C. Reheat Furnace Off-Gas Ductwork

ITC 213 ITC 100HT

Reheat furnace exhaust systems carry hot flue gases (800–1,000 deg.C+) through steel ductwork to heat recovery systems. Acid dew point corrosion attacks the cooler sections of the ductwork.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 on metal duct surfaces + ITC 100HT thermal barrier prevents acid dew point corrosion and extends ductwork life.

Reference: TXI Cement bag house — 4–5x steel shell life extension with three-coat system

8D. Roller Tables and Guides

ITC 213

Roller tables and guide equipment between the reheat furnace and rolling stands operate at high temperatures with continuous mechanical contact from hot steel. Metal components face oxidation, thermal fatigue, and wear.

ITC Solution: ITC 213 on roller table components and guides provides oxidation protection and reduces thermal fatigue, extending component life and reducing maintenance shutdowns.

Why This Matters

A single integrated steel facility like HKM represents 18 distinct application areas across all three ITC products. Each application delivers measurable ROI through one or more of:

  • Fuel savings -- 10-33% reduction in energy consumption (proven at Nucor, Arcelor Mittal, McConway and Torley)
  • Refractory life extension -- 2-5x longer campaigns (proven across all case studies)
  • Breakout prevention -- safety-critical protection for torpedo cars, ladles, and tundishes
  • Corrosion protection -- acid dew point and alkali attack prevention (proven at TXI, anchor testing)
  • Production gains -- increased throughput and yield (proven at Arcelor Mittal: $3.76M/yr total impact)

This analysis can be replicated for any integrated steel facility that Staltec calls on. The applications are universal -- only the equipment dimensions and operating conditions change.
