Facility & Challenge

Lehigh Heavy Forge is one of the largest open-die forging operations in the United States, producing massive forgings for power generation turbines, nuclear components, naval vessels, and industrial machinery. Their Bethlehem, Pennsylvania facility operates large gas-fired forging furnaces that heat steel ingots weighing from several tons up to 200+ tons to forging temperature.

The scale of operations means that even small percentage improvements in furnace efficiency translate to enormous absolute dollar savings. Specific challenges addressed:

  • Degraded ceramic fiber furnace lining with poor emissivity and energy retention
  • Extended heat-soak cycles for large ingots limiting press utilization
  • Non-uniform temperature distribution causing quality variation
  • High natural gas consumption per ton forged

ITC Solution

ITC Coatings applied ITC 100HT to the complete interior of multiple forging furnaces at the Lehigh facility. Given the size of the furnaces (some with interior volumes exceeding 5,000 cubic feet), application was performed by a dedicated ITC crew over multiple days during planned maintenance periods.

The coating strategy prioritized:

  • Roof and upper walls — primary radiant heat zones for large ingot heating
  • Door surrounds and lintels — areas of highest thermal loss
  • Side walls at work-zone height — maximizing heat transfer to ingots

Additionally, ITC 296A (top coat) was applied over ITC 100HT in high-traffic areas to provide additional surface protection and extended coating life.
