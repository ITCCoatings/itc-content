Case Study: McConway & Torley -- Austenitizing Furnace

$480,000 in Additional Annual Revenue Plus 10% Fuel Reduction

McConway & Torley, LLC -- a Pittsburgh-area manufacturer of railroad couplers and components -- faced chronic problems with their ceramic fiber-lined austenitizing furnace: shrinking insulation, hot spots, excessive fuel consumption, and long turnaround times. ITC Coatings' ceramic coating solution eliminated these issues and generated $480,000 in additional annual revenue while reducing fuel consumption by 10%.

The Challenge

The austenitizing furnace operates at approximately 1,750°F and measures 13' L x 12' W x 8' H with complete ceramic fiber lining. The sidewalls, door, and roof use 8-inch thick fiber module construction. McConway & Torley faced three major problems:

  • Heat loss and high shell temperatures -- ceramic fiber was shrinking at operating temperatures, creating gaps and hot spots on the furnace shell
  • Frequent refractory maintenance -- devitrification and shrinkage of the fiber lining required constant repair to keep the furnace operational
  • Long turnaround times -- inefficient heat distribution was extending processing cycles

ITC's Engineered Solution

ITC recommended applying a proprietary high-temperature, energy-efficient ceramic coating directly to the hot face of the ceramic fiber insulation. The project covered the entire furnace -- sidewalls, door, and roof.

The coating serves a dual purpose:

ITC coating being applied to the ceramic fiber lining inside the austenitizing furnace

Completed coating application showing uniform coverage over ceramic fiber modules

it eliminates the shrinking and devitrification that degrades ceramic fiber, while absorbing process energy and re-radiating it back to the furnace load. This keeps heat focused on the product rather than escaping through the walls.

Results

Hot Spots Eliminated

The coating stopped ceramic fiber from shrinking and devitrifying, eliminating the gaps that caused dangerous hot spots on the furnace shell. Shell temperatures dropped and became consistent across the entire structure.

46% Improvement in Temperature Uniformity

Temperature uniformity before coating: ±40°F. After coating: ±25°F. This 46% improvement means more consistent heat treatment results and higher product quality across every load.

10% Fuel Reduction

Prior to coating, fuel consumption averaged 20.8 MCF per load. After coating, consumption dropped to 18.7 MCF per load -- a 2.1 MCF reduction per load, or 10% savings on every heat treatment cycle.

3--5x Refractory Life Extension

With the coating protecting the ceramic fiber from degradation, refractory life increased 3 to 5 times its normal life expectancy, dramatically reducing both material costs and maintenance downtime.

20 Minutes Faster Turnaround -- One Extra Load Per Week

The improved thermal efficiency reduced turnaround time by 20 minutes per cycle. This seemingly small improvement added up to one additional 5-ton load processed per week.
