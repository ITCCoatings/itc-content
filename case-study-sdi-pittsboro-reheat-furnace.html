Case Study: SDI Pittsboro -- Walking Beam Reheat Furnace

$33,857 Per Year Per Skid Pipe -- Veneer System Cuts Delta T by Up to 80%

Steel Dynamics Inc. (SDI) Pittsboro's Bar Products Division operates a walking beam reheat furnace processing specialty bar quality (SBQ) steel. Excessive BTU loss through the skid pipe system was driving up fuel costs and reducing furnace efficiency. ITC Coatings engineered a veneer system that reduced skid pipe delta T temperatures by 50--80% and generated $33,857 in annual savings on a single skid pipe.

The Challenge

SDI Pittsboro's walking beam reheat furnace is rated for 125 TPH cold charge and 165 TPH hot charge, operating at 2,350°F. The facility processes plain-carbon, low-alloy, and high-alloy billets and blooms for specialty bar quality products. The primary concern was excessive BTU loss through the skid pipe system -- the water-cooled pipes that support steel as it moves through the furnace.

Skid pipes are one of the largest sources of energy loss in any reheat furnace because they constantly absorb heat from the furnace environment and transfer it to cooling water. Every BTU absorbed by a skid pipe is a BTU that doesn't go into heating the steel product.

ITC's Engineered Solution

Using heat flow calculations, ITC designed a hot face veneer system for the skid pipes consisting of:

  • 1-inch thick, 12" x 12" ceramic fiber squares applied to skid pipes
  • A proprietary high-temperature, energy-efficient ceramic (HTEEC) hot face coating

The veneer system was installed September 4--6, 2012, and the furnace was started up September 7, 2012. Temperature readings were taken March 1, 2013 -- approximately six months into operation -- confirming the system was performing as designed.

Measured Results

Engineering layout of the SDI Pittsboro walking beam reheat furnace skid pipe system showing veneered areas

Delta T Reductions of 50--80%

Detailed temperature measurements were taken across the entire furnace -- north, center, and south sides in both the charge/heat zone and the soak zone. The results confirmed dramatic reductions in skid pipe delta T temperatures:

  • Charge/Heat Zone: Delta T readings as low as 5°F on veneered monorails (previously 26--38°F range)
  • Soak Zone (hottest zone of furnace): Delta T readings of 7--13°F on fully veneered skids and posts
  • Fixed Skids: Consistent performance across all zones

The furnace was operating at a set point of 2,345°F with skid flow rates averaging 295 seconds per billet, running multiple grades of steel at different bar lengths -- real-world production conditions, not laboratory tests.

BTU Savings Exceeded Estimates

Due to the thermal efficiencies achieved, actual BTU savings were significantly better than the original engineering estimates. The veneer and coating combination kept more energy in the furnace and directed it toward heating the steel load.

Reduced Refractory Maintenance

The veneer system protected the skid pipe refractory shapes from thermal shock and heat-related stresses, reducing overall maintenance requirements and extending the service life of the skid pipe system.

The $33,857 annual savings figure applies to one horizontal movable skid pipe. A typical walking beam reheat furnace has multiple skid pipes -- scaling this solution across the complete skid system multiplies the savings proportionally.

Why Skid Pipe Veneering Matters

Skid pipes are often overlooked as an energy savings opportunity because they're seen as a fixed part of the furnace design. But the data from SDI Pittsboro proves that a veneer and coating system can dramatically reduce the energy absorbed by the cooling water system, returning that energy to the steel product.
