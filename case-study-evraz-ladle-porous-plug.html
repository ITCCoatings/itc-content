Facility & Challenge

EVRAZ Rocky Mountain Steel in Pueblo, Colorado operates rail and structural steel production using electric arc furnaces and ladle metallurgy furnaces (LMF). The ladle stirring system uses porous plugs installed in the ladle bottom — argon gas flows through the porous plug to stir and homogenize the liquid steel.

The porous plug extractor tool — used to remove porous plugs from the ladle after campaigns — was experiencing severe operational problems:

  • Steel bonding: Solidified steel and slag would bond the extractor to the ladle bottom, making removal difficult or impossible
  • Thermal damage: Repeated exposure to liquid steel temperatures (2,900°F+) was cracking and degrading the extractor
  • Seizure events: Frequent seizure of the extractor required emergency intervention and caused production delays
  • Short service life: Extractors required frequent replacement due to thermal and chemical damage

ITC Solution

ITC Coatings developed an application protocol for ITC 213 specifically for the porous plug extractor environment. The coating was applied to the contact surfaces of the extractor tool, creating a non-stick, heat-resistant ceramic barrier between the tool and the molten steel environment.

ITC 213's key properties for this application:

  • Non-wetting to molten steel at ladle operating temperatures
  • Excellent thermal shock resistance from repeated insertion/removal cycles
  • Adheres to the steel extractor substrate even under thermal cycling
  • Prevents steel and slag from bonding to the tool surface
