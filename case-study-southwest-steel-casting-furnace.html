Facility & Challenge

Southwest Steel Casting Co. operates a heat treat furnace approximately 13' L × 8' W × 6' H for the heat treatment of steel castings. The furnace features a complete ceramic fiber lining — sidewalls are 8" thick stack bonded fiber construction, and the roof is 8" thick fiber module construction.

Challenges included:

  • Ceramic fiber shrinkage and devitrification at operating temperature — degrading insulation performance
  • Elevated shell temperatures and hot spots indicating heat loss through the lining
  • High frequency of refractory maintenance — time and material costs eroding profitability
  • Extended turn-around time after maintenance outages, reducing tons produced per week

ITC Solution

ITC 100HT

ITC 100HT high-temperature ceramic coating was applied to the hot face of the complete ceramic fiber lining — sidewalls, roof, and door. The coating prevents the fiber from shrinking and devitrifying, while reflecting radiant heat back into the working zone to improve thermal efficiency.
