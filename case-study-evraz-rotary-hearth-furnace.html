Facility & Challenge

EVRAZ Rocky Mountain Steel's Pueblo facility is a major producer of steel rail for North American railroads, as well as structural steel products. The rotary hearth reheat furnace is a critical link in the production process, heating steel blooms to rolling temperature before they enter the rail rolling mill.

The large rotary hearth furnace (rotating hearth design allowing continuous charging and discharging) presents significant energy costs due to:

  • Enormous furnace interior surface area requiring consistent thermal management
  • Continuous operation at high temperatures (2,200–2,300°F) demands efficient energy use
  • Degraded refractory lining surfaces with poor emissivity characteristics
  • Scale buildup on hearth surfaces reducing heat transfer efficiency
  • Energy costs representing one of the facility's largest operating expense categories

ITC Solution

ITC Coatings recommended a comprehensive application of ITC 100HT throughout the furnace interior — walls, roof arch, and hearth surfaces — during a planned annual maintenance shutdown.

The application strategy covered:

  • Roof and walls: High-emissivity coating dramatically increases radiant heat transfer to the steel charge
  • Hearth surface: Coating protects against scale adhesion and improves heat transmission to billets from below
  • Burner zones: Enhanced emissivity creates more efficient combustion atmosphere

Total coating area exceeded 15,000 square feet. Application was completed by ITC field crew in 3 days during the shutdown window.
