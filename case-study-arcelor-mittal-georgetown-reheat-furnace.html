Facility & Challenge

ArcelorMittal Georgetown Steel operates a wire rod rolling mill in Georgetown, South Carolina. The facility uses a walking beam reheat furnace to bring steel billets to rolling temperature (approximately 2,200–2,300°F) prior to the rolling process.

Over time, the ceramic fiber lining of the reheat furnace had degraded, leading to:

  • Excessive heat loss through the furnace walls and roof
  • Increased natural gas consumption to maintain target soak temperatures
  • Fiber erosion from high-velocity burner flame impingement
  • Reduced furnace efficiency and throughput capacity limitations

The plant was seeking a cost-effective way to restore furnace efficiency without a full ceramic fiber replacement, which would require an extended shutdown.

ITC Solution

ITC Coatings recommended coating the entire furnace interior — walls, roof, and floor — with ITC 100HT, a high-emissivity ceramic coating specifically engineered for refractory and ceramic fiber applications.

ITC 100HT works through two mechanisms:

  • High emissivity (0.92+): Radiates stored heat back into the furnace chamber, reducing the energy needed to maintain temperature
  • Surface hardener: Seals and protects degraded ceramic fiber surfaces, preventing fiber erosion and reducing heat loss through exposed fiber matrix

The coating was applied by spray during a scheduled maintenance outage. Total application time was under 24 hours for the complete furnace interior.
