Case Study: Salt River Materials Group -- Cement Kiln Burner Pipe

$33,374 Annual Savings with a 3.1 Month ROI

Salt River Materials Group's Arizona cement plant was relining their kiln burner pipe every four months due to thermal shock from cycling the kiln. ITC Coatings' ceramic coating solution extended refractory life 2--3x longer, cut annual refractory costs by two-thirds, and paid for itself in just 3.1 months.

The Challenge

The cement kiln burner pipe at Salt River Materials Group is 26 feet long and approximately 30 inches in diameter. The existing lining configuration consisted of a ¼-inch layer of ceramic fiber blanket with 3 inches of RESCO's R-Max CZ high-performance castable.

The primary challenge was continual refractory failure from thermal shock. Every time the kiln cycled (heating and cooling), the thermal stress degraded the castable lining. On average, the refractory lasted approximately 4 months before requiring a complete reline -- meaning three full relines per year at significant cost and downtime.

ITC's Engineered Solution

Engineering cross-sections of the cement kiln burner pipe showing refractory lining and ITC coating zones

ITC recommended applying ITC 100HT -- a proprietary high-emissivity hot face coating -- directly to the hot face of the R-Max CZ castable. No veneer system was required; the coating alone addressed the root cause.

ITC 100HT provides two critical functions on a cement kiln burner pipe:

  • Thermal shock protection: The coating protects the substrate refractory from the cyclical heating and cooling that causes cracking and spalling
  • Reduced heat transfer: Minimizes heat loss through the refractory, which also reduces oxidation of the burner pipe and premature failure of internal burner components

Results

Refractory Life Extended 2--3x

With ITC 100HT protecting the hot face, refractory life improved from the previous 4-month average to 12 months or longer. The coating eliminated the thermal shock damage that had been causing premature failure.

Why This Matters for Cement and Lime Producers

Cement kiln burner pipes operate in one of the most demanding thermal environments in any industrial process. The combination of extreme temperatures, thermal cycling, and chemical attack from kiln gases makes refractory failure a constant and expensive reality.
