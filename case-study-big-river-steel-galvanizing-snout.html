Facility & Challenge

Big River Steel operates a state-of-the-art continuous galvanizing line (CGL) in Osceola, Arkansas. The galvanizing snout is the critical component that seals the annealing furnace atmosphere from the molten zinc bath, directing the steel strip into the zinc at the proper angle and atmosphere.

The snout presented a persistent operational challenge:

  • Zinc dross buildup: Molten zinc and iron-zinc compounds accumulated on the interior snout surfaces, requiring frequent manual cleaning interventions
  • Production interruptions: Snout cleaning required line stops, reducing throughput and uptime
  • Surface defects: Dross particles falling onto the strip surface caused coating defects and customer rejections
  • Component wear: Zinc attack on the snout material accelerated component degradation

Standard stainless steel snout surfaces had poor release characteristics with molten zinc, making dross adhesion and buildup nearly continuous.

ITC Solution

ITC Coatings applied ITC 213 to the interior surfaces of the galvanizing snout. ITC 213 creates a non-wetting ceramic surface that molten zinc and dross cannot adhere to effectively.

The coating mechanism works by:

  • Creating a chemically inert surface that zinc oxides and iron-zinc intermetallics cannot bond to
  • Providing excellent thermal stability at zinc bath temperatures (840–860°F)
  • Resisting the aggressive chemical environment of the zinc atmosphere transition zone
  • Maintaining non-stick surface characteristics through thermal cycling

  • ITC 213 non-wetting ceramic coating reduced snout dross buildup by approximately 75%
  • Snout cleaning frequency reduced by ~60%, recovering significant production uptime
  • Strip surface quality improved with fewer dross-related defects
  • ITC 213 withstands the unique chemical and thermal environment of the CGL snout
  • Simple application process compatible with snout geometry and maintenance windows
  • Solution eliminates root cause of dross adhesion rather than just treating the symptom
