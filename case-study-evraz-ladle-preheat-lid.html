Facility & Challenge

EVRAZ Rocky Mountain Steel operates ladle preheating stations where steel ladles are heated prior to receiving liquid steel from the EAF. The preheat lid is a large ceramic fiber-lined enclosure that covers the ladle opening during preheating, directing burner flame and retained heat to warm the ladle refractory to the target preheat temperature (typically 2,000–2,400°F).

Two problems drove the investigation of ITC coatings for the preheat lid:

  • High fuel consumption: The ceramic fiber lining was degraded and had low emissivity, absorbing heat poorly and radiating it back inefficiently
  • Premature lid failure: The fiber lining was eroding from burner impingement and thermal cycling, requiring frequent and costly lid replacements
  • Extended preheat cycles: Inefficient heat transfer meant longer preheat times, creating scheduling bottlenecks

ITC Solution

ITC 100HT high-emissivity ceramic coating was applied to the entire interior surface of the preheat lid ceramic fiber lining. ITC 100HT transforms the degraded low-emissivity fiber into a high-emissivity (~0.92) radiating surface that efficiently directs absorbed energy back into the ladle cavity.

Additionally, ITC 100HT:

  • Seals and hardens the degraded ceramic fiber surface, protecting against further erosion
  • Resists direct burner flame impingement that was destroying the base fiber
  • Maintains effectiveness through hundreds of thermal cycles
  • Applied by spray in under 2 hours per lid during routine maintenance
