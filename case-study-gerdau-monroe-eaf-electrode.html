Facility & Challenge

Gerdau Monroe operates an electric arc furnace melting facility in Monroe, Michigan, producing merchant bar quality (MBQ) and special bar quality (SBQ) steel for diverse end markets. The facility runs high-productivity EAF operations with aggressive power-on time targets.

Electrode consumption at Gerdau Monroe was a focus for cost reduction due to:

  • High-intensity operation driving above-average electrode wear rates
  • Increasing electrode costs from global supply constraints
  • Side-wall oxidation identified as the primary consumption mechanism above the slag line
  • Potential for significant savings given the volume of electrodes consumed annually

The Monroe team implemented a rigorous three-month trial to quantify the true impact of ITC 213 under production conditions.

ITC Solution

ITC 213 was applied by brush to electrode side walls above the anticipated slag line prior to each heat during the trial period. A dedicated operator applied the coating during the approximately 5-minute electrode positioning window between heats.

Key aspects of the trial design:

  • Baseline consumption data collected for 30 days prior to coating trial
  • ITC 213 applied consistently to all three electrodes each heat
  • Consumption data tracked by heat and compared to baseline
  • Electrode supplier and grade held constant throughout trial

Key Takeaways

  • Gerdau Monroe's three-month trial demonstrated a statistically significant 18% electrode consumption reduction
  • Annual savings of $350,000 documented through rigorous before/after measurement
  • Trial methodology provides compelling, data-driven business case for continued use
  • No changes to furnace operation, heat practice, or electrode specifications required
  • The 5-minute application time per heat is fully compatible with normal furnace cycle time
  • Gerdau Monroe subsequently implemented ITC 213 as standard operating procedure
