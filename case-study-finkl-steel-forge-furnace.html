Facility & Challenge

Finkl Steel is North America's largest producer of tool steel and specialty forgings, supplying critical components to the automotive, plastics, mining, and energy industries. Their Chicago facility operates multiple forge furnaces that heat large steel ingots and billets to forging temperatures prior to press operations.

The forge furnaces use ceramic fiber lining for rapid heat-up capability and fuel efficiency. Over years of operation, the ceramic fiber had degraded:

  • Surface erosion from thermal cycling and gas flow
  • Fiber sintering and shrinkage reducing insulation value
  • Low emissivity surface failing to return absorbed heat to the work
  • Increased fuel consumption and extended heat-soak cycles
  • Temperature non-uniformity affecting forging consistency

ITC Solution

ITC Coatings applied ITC 100HT to all interior surfaces of the forge furnaces — including the roof, walls, and door. The project was timed to coincide with a scheduled forge press maintenance window, minimizing production impact.

ITC 100HT provides two critical benefits for forge furnace environments:

  1. High emissivity (0.92+): The ceramic fiber naturally has low emissivity (~0.30). Coating with ITC 100HT increases emissivity to over 0.92, dramatically improving radiant heat transfer to the steel work
  2. Surface hardener: Seals and strengthens the degraded fiber surface, extending lining life and preventing particulate contamination of the work surface
