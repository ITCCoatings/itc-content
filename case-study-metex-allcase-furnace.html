Facility & Challenge

Metex operates an Allcase radiant tube oil quench heat treat furnace in Ontario, Canada, running at 1,600°F. The furnace is used to heat treat various steel parts — a continuous, high-cycle operation where furnace uptime and energy efficiency are critical.

Key challenges included:

  • High fuel consumption from heat loss through uncoated refractory linings
  • Frequent refractory repair and maintenance — ceramic fiber shrinkage and degradation
  • Extended preheat times after shutdowns, reducing productive hours
  • Radiant tube oxidation and degradation requiring repeated weld repair and replacement

ITC Solution

ITC 100HT ITC 213

ITC applied a dual-product system targeting both the refractory lining and the metal components:

  • ITC 100HT — Applied to the hot face of the insulating firebrick (IFB) lining. Reflects radiant heat back into the working zone, prevents fiber shrinkage and devitrification, and creates a hard protective barrier that extends refractory life.
  • ITC 213 — Applied to the radiant tubes. Protects the metal tubes from oxidation, thermal fatigue, and surface degradation. Extends tube life and eliminates repeated weld repair cycles.

Key Takeaways

  • 30% fuel savings from ITC 100HT reflecting radiant heat back into the working zone — direct bottom-line impact
  • 2–3x refractory life — eliminates the continuous repair cycle and reduces labor and material costs
  • 50% reduction in preheat time — faster recovery from shutdowns means more productive hours per week
  • 2x longer radiant tube life from ITC 213 — eliminates repeated weld repair, reduces downtime
  • Dual-product approach (100HT on refractory + 213 on metal) maximizes ROI across all furnace components
  • Same results applicable to any allcase, batch integral quench (BIQ), or radiant tube heat treat furnace
