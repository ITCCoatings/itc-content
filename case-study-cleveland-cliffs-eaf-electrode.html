Facility & Challenge

Cleveland-Cliffs' Mansfield facility operates a high-powered electric arc furnace producing hot-rolled coil for automotive and industrial markets. The EAF employs large graphite electrodes to deliver massive electrical energy to the steel scrap charge — a process that inherently subjects the electrodes to extreme oxidation, chemical attack, and thermal shock.

Electrode consumption represents one of the highest variable costs in EAF steelmaking, typically ranging from $3–6/ton of steel produced. The Mansfield facility was experiencing electrode consumption rates that offered significant cost reduction opportunity through technological intervention.

Key cost drivers included:

  • Side-wall oxidation above the slag line — consuming electrode mass without contributing electrical energy
  • Chemical attack from furnace off-gases and reactive furnace atmosphere
  • High-intensity operations shortening electrode service life

ITC Solution

ITC 213 ceramic coating was applied to electrode side walls by brush prior to each furnace charge. The coating creates a continuous oxygen-barrier layer on the graphite surface, dramatically reducing oxidation-driven consumption.

ITC 213 Properties:

  • Bonds permanently to graphite at temperature
  • Creates alumina-silica barrier impermeable to oxygen at furnace temperatures
  • Withstands thermal shock during scrap charging and tap operations
  • Applied cold — no pre-heating required
  • Water-based, environmentally safe application
